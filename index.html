📱 Family Chores

Quick Start

Open App Info: Click the sidebar ℹ️ App Info button to read these notes and find quick links.

Get started: Add family members with ➕ Add Member, create chores on the board, assign owners, then mark chores done to earn stars.

If stuck: Use the scroll buttons (▲/▼) in this modal to move through the content.

Dashboard At A Glance

  • Board View: The main screen is a kanban-style board showing chores grouped by time or category — drag and drop to reorder.
  • Member column: Each family member has a column (avatar + name); chores listed beneath show who is responsible.
  • Top widgets: Small panels display weather, date/time, and quick-action buttons (Weekly Summary, Progress Chart).
  • Sidebar: Use the sidebar buttons (📅 Family Calendar, 📊 Weekly Summary, 📈 Progress Chart, 🎁 Reward Store) to open related tools and reports.

Key Interface Elements

  • ➕ Add Member: Opens the Add Member popup to create a new family profile (name, color, optional avatar).
  • Chore cards: Tap a chore to view details, set difficulty, add photo proof, or mark it complete.
  • 📅 Family Calendar: Shows chores with dates and recurrence; useful for planning.
  • 📊 Weekly Summary: Quick exportable view of weekly stars and completions.
  • 📈 Progress Chart: Visual trend of chores and stars over time to track improvement.

Creating a Chore (4 steps)

  1. Step 1 — Add: Use the + Add Chore control on a member/column or via the UI control you use to create chores.
  2. Step 2 — Name & Details: Enter title, optional notes, and choose difficulty (Easy/Medium/Hard).
  3. Step 3 — Assign & Schedule: Assign to a family member, set a one-time date or recurrence (daily/weekly/rrule).
  4. Step 4 — Save & Notify: Save the chore; assigned members see it immediately and will receive reminders if enabled.

Types of Tasks

  • One-off tasks: Single date; disappears after completion.
  • Recurring tasks: Repeat on selected days or using advanced rules (RRULE); the calendar will show future occurrences.
  • Timed vs. flexible: Some chores are date-specific (e.g., “Take out trash”), others are flexible tasks to complete anytime.

Assigning & Managing

  • Change assignee: Open the chore card and choose another member.
  • Reassigning on repeat: For recurring chores you can keep the same assignee or set rotating assignments (use notes or sequence options).
  • Reordering: Drag chore cards within a column to prioritize.

Understanding Analytics

  • Completed chores: Total number finished in the selected period (day/week/month).
  • Completion rate: Percentage of assigned chores completed vs. assigned. Use this to see reliability per person.
  • Stars earned: Reward currency earned for completing chores (seen in 🎁 Reward Store).
  • Trend chart: 📈 Progress Chart shows changes over time — rising lines = more chores completed / more stars awarded.
  • Member breakdown: Use 📊 Weekly Summary to see each member’s totals and to export CSV for record-keeping.

How to Read the Charts

  • X axis: Time (days/weeks/months).
  • Y axis: Quantity (chores or stars).
  • Peaks: Days with many completions — check what worked and repeat.
  • Drops: Low activity — consider reminders, reassigning, or adjusting difficulty.

Rewards & Stars

  • Earning: Completing chores awards stars based on difficulty.
  • Redeeming: Open 🎁 Reward Store to trade stars for rewards set by the family.
  • Tracking: Redeemed items and star balances are shown under Rewards and in the Weekly Summary.

Notifications & Exports

  • Reminders: Enable notifications for chore reminders; check browser/device notification permissions.
  • Export CSV / iCal: Use 📊 Weekly Summary to export a CSV and 📅 Family Calendar to export iCal for calendar apps.
  • Photo proof: Use the Photo Proof modal to attach a photo when a chore requires evidence.

Privacy & Storage

  • Local-first: Data is stored in your browser (localStorage) by default so you keep control.
  • Cloud sync (optional): Signing in saves a snapshot to Firestore under users/{uid} (writes require a verified email).
  • Payments: Stripe handles subscriptions; no card data is stored on this client.

Common Questions (Quick FAQ)

  • Q: How do I mark a chore done?
    A: Tap the chore card and press the complete/check action (or use the daily logs). Completed chores update the charts and award stars.
  • Q: A chore repeats but didn’t reappear — why?
    A: Check the chore’s recurrence settings (weekly days or RRULE). Also confirm local time/date and the calendar view.
  • Q: How do I change difficulty or stars value?
    A: Open the chore, edit difficulty (Easy/Medium/Hard). Stars awarded update automatically based on difficulty.
  • Q: How can I give premium access to a friend?
    A: Premium is granted via subscription; small client-side whitelist or server claims may also be used — contact account admin/support.
  • Q: How do I recover data on a new device?
    A: Sign in with the same account, verify email, then load your user snapshot from the cloud sync if previously saved.
  • Q: I don’t see notifications — what to check?
    A: Ensure browser and OS notifications are enabled and that you allowed notifications when prompted.

Troubleshooting Tips

  • If something disappears: Refresh the page and check both localStorage and the signed-in user snapshot if you use cloud sync.
  • Slow chart updates: Charts update after actions — try opening/closing the chart modal or reloading to force a refresh.
  • Auth/Firestore errors: Email verification is required for cloud writes; verify your account via the verification email.

Quick UX Tips

  • Use drag & drop to prioritize chores — top = higher priority.
  • Set clear rewards in the Reward Store to motivate kids and keep the system fun.
  • Weekly exports keep a family ledger and help discuss chores during family meetings.

Accessibility & Family-Friendly Advice

  • Readable text: Use the larger font settings (browser zoom) if needed — the UI is keyboard accessible for many actions.
  • For younger children: Use emojis and short chore titles, and rely on stars/rewards to gamify tasks.
  • For less tech-savvy members: Assign simple, fixed chores and use weekly summaries in family meetings.
